Antwerp, 1550

As an employee of Plantin you’ll be working in the Officina Plantiniana, his publishing house located in Antwerp. Plantin who was born in France moved here around 1550 to develop his printing business. But why Antwerp?

Antwerp offered opportunities since it was one of the wealthiest and most cosmopolitan cities in Europe during the mid-16th century. It was a major commercial hub, with thriving trade and a high demand for books, maps, and other printed materials, making it an ideal place for a printer and publisher.

Building the page
the compositor's job

compositor job description

Your task as a compositor is to carefully select lead letters from the type case, arranging them in the correct order to form a complete line of text. These lines are then combined to create a column or an entire page, known as a ‘forme’ which is used for printing onto a sheet of paper.

To uphold his reputation, Plantin carefully selects typefaces. Initially using common fonts, he soon acquires more exclusive designs from French masters like Garamont and Granjon.

Make sure the prints remain their quality by securing the loose lead letters in place.

Building the page
the printer's job

printer job description

Before any book comes to life, it must pass through your hands as the printer, transforming ink and paper into lasting works of art. Your skill at the press is vital to ensuring each page is clear and worthy of the Plantin name.

Your job as a printer is to lock the forms in the press and ink them with semi-circular spheres: the ink balls or dabbers. You will then insert the sheet of paper and roll the carriage into position. A heavy bar needs to be pulled to produce a printed sheet. It’s important to apply a steady pressure while pulling to ensure a clean and even result. Finally, the printed paper is removed and hung up to dry. This is repeated some 2500 times a day.
